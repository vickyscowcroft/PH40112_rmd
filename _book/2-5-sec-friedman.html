2.5 Friedman Equation

Using the time-time solution of the Einstein equation gives \[\begin{equation} \left(\dfrac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 + \dfrac{kc^2}{a^2} = \dfrac{8\pi G}{3}\rho \tag{1.15} \end{equation}\] which is the Friedman equation.

The space-space solution follows in a similar way:

\[\begin{equation} 2\dfrac{\ddot{a}}{a} + \left(\dfrac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 + \dfrac{kc^2}{a^2} = -8\pi G \dfrac{P}{c^2} \tag{1.16} \end{equation}\]

Which looks odd right now, but by subtracting the Friedman equation (Eqn. (1.15)) we find: \[\begin{equation} \dfrac{\ddot{a}}{a} = -\dfrac{4\pi G}{3}\left(\rho + \dfrac{3P}{c^2}\right) \tag{1.17} \end{equation}\]

which is the known as the acceleration equation.
